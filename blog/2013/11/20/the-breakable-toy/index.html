The Breakable Toy

Nov 20th, 2013

Today I began working on one of the most interesting parts of Launch Academy, the breakable toy. It’s an application of our own choosing that we will build, then break and rebuild as part of the program. The idea is to have a place where we can put the concepts we’re learning into a real project. It’s our first exposure to full-stack programming and the design and development of software.

In choosing my breakable toy I wanted to build something that solved a problem that hadn’t been solved with software. I decided to build a program that matches patients on a standby list (to see a doctor/dentist) with cancelled appointments they are available for. I wanted something simple so I don’t want to integrate/interfere with current scheduling systems. I’ve worked in a doctor’s office before and from talking to some in the industry cancellations and double bookings are a big problem this may help alleviate.

The program is quite straightforward. The patient availability will be entered into the system. When there is a cancellation, it will be entered into the system. They system will rank the available patients for that timeslot, and the first to confirm will be assigned that appointment time.

There are 2 components to the application I feel will be the most challenging. The matching system needs to be able to message multiple users to make sure that it fills the spot quickly, but in a way that gives users time to respond and doesn’t alienate those that don’t respond quickly. Secondly, the users will most likely be sent an email link to confirm, but ideally they could do so by SMS or email directly.

Right now I’ve only begun writing user stories, but the breakable toy is a great way to go through the development cycle from planning to deployment in the time we have here at Launch Academy.
