Launch Academy: Week 1 Synopsis

Nov 18th, 2013

The first week at Launch Academy has come to a close, and I know I’ve learned a lot because I begin the second week with more questions than I had coming in. I started the course with some basic ruby knowledge, in one week I’m ready to tackle some tougher problems and start writing more advanced ruby in a object oriented way. The instructors have been great, the talks have really helped give context to a lot of stuff I had tried to learn myself and didn’t really understand; and I’m getting comfortable navigating my development environment with the command line, sublime text, and pry. I find myself learning an order of magnitude faster because there’s always someone to help explain a concept when I get stuck and the energy level and motivation of my peers keeps me going.

What really struck me about my Launch Academy experience is how much I enjoyed it despite the intensity and hours required to keep up. I’ve always loved learning and have had to cram for exams before, but this is nothing like those University finals weeks. Firstly, I’m not graded or ranked, so rather than gaming the system to try and get a passing mark the only thing that matters is the quality of my code. This fundamental shift changes the motivation for hard work and success from extrinsic to intrinsic. I find myself putting in 12+ hour days and rather than feeling burned out it has positive effects on the other aspects of my daily life. Even with a 70 hour plus workweek I find myself at the gym more often and waking up earlier. Doesn’t hurt that everyone else in the room is having the same great experience.

The collaborative atmosphere in which work gets done is the icing on the cake. People often avoid working with computers because they feel they aren’t suited to sit in front of a computer all day. That isn’t what it’s like at all. After having worked on a construction site, mine, factory, restaurant, office, and car wash; I can definitely say that if working as a developer is anything like Launch Academy, it is one of the best work environments that exists. After attending a Boston Ruby meetup this week and Dan’s talk on Agile development I realize this is not something that will end in 10 weeks but will be part of the culture at many of the companies in this industry.

I head into the second week looking forward to improving my object oriented Ruby skills, and seeing what else Dan and the team teach me about the industry and the subject matter. It’s only been a week but I definitely feel I’ve come to the right place.
