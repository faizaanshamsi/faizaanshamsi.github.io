Alpha Complete!

Nov 25th, 2013

The first phase of Launch Academy has come to a close, and I’m supposed to be reflecting on a specific concept that I found difficult. To be honest, the greatest challenge over these past two weeks has been managing time and energy effectively. With our work weeks exceeding 70 hours, trying to find a good balance between work, sleep, excercise, and time to unwind has been difficult. When I’ve compromised on any one of those factors, the quality and pace at which I learn and work is appreciably affected. As I enter the second phase, here are some lessons I have learned about how I work best.

Excercise: Other than actually coding itself, this is the single greatest factor in how efficient and alert I remain through the day. Our days at Launch Academy are long, 12 hours are often required to get the most out of the day. A good workout in the morning is the key to staying mentally alert, and physically tired enough to fall asleep early and get a good night’s sleep. The days I skimp on this I suffer.

Pairing: I was extremely resistant to the idea of pairing as I felt it slowed me down. This is especially relevant since the amount of time we have to spend just to keep up means any time wasted could have us falling behind. My opinion on pairing remained pretty constant until we approached the end of the phase. As our projects grew bigger and started to span over multiple days having a partner to help organize, troubleshoot, and just keep motivation up in general had tons of value. I can definitely see where it will matter as we start Rails and have assignments with longer timelines.

Planning: According to some of my peers, I have a tendency to just “spaz” code out when solving a problem. This was fine in the early part of the phase when our problems were simple and our code short, but as our assignments have grown more elaborate this methodology (or lack of) comes with a lot of technical debt. Once again, I found myself initially resistant to whiteboarding and planning but now realize it’s essential to plan out how you’re modelling your data before you begin. Looking forward to learn databases and ActiveRecord so I never have to store data in a .csv again.

We’ll see what phase 2 brings, Launch Academy is forcing me to start picking up good habits that will stay with me after the program is done in eight weeks (that soon!).
