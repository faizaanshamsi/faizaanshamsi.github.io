Why I’m at Launch Academy

Nov 5th, 2013

I had been frustrated with University, after working multiple internships and switching majors I found myself still unsatisfied with the path I was on and not motivated academically. Finally, in the summer of 2011 I was looking to do something different and I left for India. I spend the summer building a computer lab from scratch amidst monsoons, extreme heat, and frequent power outages. I taught the students to build computers, use them, and access the internet for the first time. I taught some english and math using Khan Academy and the philosophy of the “flipped classroom” to great effect. The summer was transformational, and I unearthed a passion for technology, creative problem solving, and teaching.

The following year, some friends and I decided we wanted to start a business, and our idea happened to be software based. I felt the intensity and problems solving inherent in starting a business would best replicate the sort of experience I had in India. None of us had a software background but we felt being in a university town we’d easily find developers if our idea was good. To make a very long story short, it was incredibly difficult to find/retain talent with no funds, and repeatedly delaying simple tasks like a decent landing page left me and my co founder frustrated enough to start learning HTML and CSS on our own. I touched upon Ruby and Rails from some other resources and I was hooked.

I’m at Launch Academy because it will help me become a great junior Ruby on Rails Developer. Even more importantly, it puts me on a path where creativity, problem solving, technology, and learning or teaching new things are part of the job description.
